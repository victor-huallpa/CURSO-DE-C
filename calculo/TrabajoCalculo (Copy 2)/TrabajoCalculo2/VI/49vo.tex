$y=\ln(\cos x)$ desde $x=\pi/6$ a $x=\frac{1}{4} \pi$.

\nopagebreak
\begin{align*}
y' = \frac{-\sin x}{\cos x} = -\tan x. \\
1+(y')^2 = 1+\tan^2 x = \sec^2 x. \\
L &= \int_{\pi/6}^{\pi/4} \sec x \, dx = [\ln|\sec x + \tan x|]_{\pi/6}^{\pi/4} \\
&= \ln(\sqrt{2}+1) - \ln(2/\sqrt{3} + 1/\sqrt{3}) \\
&= \ln(\sqrt{2}+1) - \ln(\sqrt{3}) = \ln\left(\frac{\sqrt{2}+1}{\sqrt{3}}\right)
\end{align*}

\[
\boxed{\displaystyle \ln\left(\frac{\sqrt{2}+1}{\sqrt{3}}\right)}
